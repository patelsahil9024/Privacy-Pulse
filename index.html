Introduction

In today's digital age, college students are increasingly exposed to online platforms, social media, and data-sharing services. However, awareness of data privacy remains low, leading to risks such as identity theft, data breaches, and unauthorized surveillance. This website aims to educate and raise awareness about importance of data privacy among college students.

Objectives

  • To inform college students about basics of data privacy and its implications.
  • To provide practical tips for protecting personal data online.
  • To encourage responsible digital behavior through awareness campaigns.
  • To analyze current awareness levels and propose improvements.

Scope of Awareness

The scope of this awareness initiative covers various aspects of data privacy, including social media usage, online shopping, educational platforms, and mobile app permissions. It targets college students globally, focusing on common risks like phishing, data mining, and privacy policy ignorance.

Target Audience: College Students

Research Findings

Abstract

This research investigates level of data privacy awareness among college students. Through surveys and data analysis, we found that while students are active online, many lack understanding of privacy settings and risks. Key findings include a 60% awareness gap in social media privacy controls.

Safety Practices

  • Regular Audits: Conduct a monthly audit of your connected apps and revoke access for services you no longer use.
  • Browsing Habits: Always look for "https://" in the URL bar before entering sensitive data. Avoid downloading attachments from unknown senders.
  • Device Security: Ensure your laptop and mobile devices are protected with strong PINs or biometrics.
  • Backup Data: Maintain regular backups of important data to protect against ransomware attacks.

⚠️ CASE STUDY University Credential Stuffing Attack

In 2023, a major university experienced a targeted credential stuffing attack. Hackers utilized username and password combinations leaked from third-party breaches to gain access to student and staff email portals. Once inside, they scraped for sensitive research data and financial aid records. This case highlights the critical risk of password reuse across different platforms and the absolute necessity of enabling Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on educational accounts. It serves as a stark reminder that a breach on one site can compromise accounts on many others.

Social Media Awareness Posters

1. Physical Security

  • Be Cautious with Office Waste: Trash may contain important information.
  • Avoid throwing sensitive documents into standard waste bags.
  • Always ensure physical media is securely destroyed.

2. Digital Citizenship

  • Privacy: Keep private information private.
  • Passwords: Use passwords and protect them.
  • Think Before You Click: Be mindful of your online actions.

3. Lock Down Your Login

  • Multi-Factor Authentication: Add an extra layer of security.
  • Unique Passwords: Do not reuse passwords for different sites.
  • Login Notifications: Enable alerts to detect unauthorized access immediately.

4. Online Data Protection

  • Automatic Updates: Keep software current to patch vulnerabilities.
  • Disaster Recovery: Have a plan for data loss.
  • Anti-Spyware: Protect against malicious spying software.
  • Universal File Access: Securely access files from anywhere.

5. Protect Personal Info

"Your Personal Information is Valuable: Protect It"


  • Give Your Phone a Fresh Start: Disguise your phone name.
  • Spruce Up Your Social: Toss out those old apps you don't use.
  • Clean up your digital footprint regularly.

6. General Awareness

  • Report Suspicious Activity: Immediately notify IT of any odd behavior.
  • Lock Your Screen: Always lock your workstation when stepping away.
  • Stay Informed: Keep up to date with latest security policies.
